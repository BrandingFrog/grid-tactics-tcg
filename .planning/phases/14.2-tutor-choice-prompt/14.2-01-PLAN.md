<encoding_decision>
**Action-space layout [0:1262] is PRESERVED.** No resize. Mirrors 14.1 strategy.

Encoding:
1. New enum values `ActionType.TUTOR_SELECT = 9` and `ActionType.DECLINE_TUTOR = 10` (Python semantic only; they do NOT consume fresh action-space slots).
2. `TUTOR_SELECT` reuses the PLAY_CARD[0:250] slot space, reinterpreted while `pending_tutor_player_idx is not None`: slot i in [0:250] means "select deck card at match-index i" (bounded by len(pending_tutor_matches), typically <= 3 for current cards; plenty of headroom). Rationale: PLAY_CARD is the natural "pick a card" channel and PLAY_CARD is illegal during pending_tutor anyway.
3. `DECLINE_TUTOR` reuses slot 1001 (PASS), reinterpreted as decline while pending_tutor is set — same trick as 14.1's DECLINE_POST_MOVE_ATTACK. Regular PASS as turn-end is illegal during pending_tutor.
4. Only ONE pending flavor active at a time: tutor fires from on_play (card resolution path), melee pending fires from MOVE. They share slot 1001 but the active pending field disambiguates.
5. Rationale: Zero RL checkpoint invalidation. Preserves 14.1 encoding. Keeps slot math trivial.

**Tensor parity:** `pending_tutor_player_idx` becomes `[N]` int tensor, sentinel -1. `pending_tutor_matches` becomes `[N, K]` int tensor (K = max tutor matches, pick K=8 for headroom), sentinel -1 for empty slots. Mirrored in Wave 2.

**RL training trade-off:** Tensor engine exposes TUTOR_SELECT as a real sub-action (parity with Python). No auto-resolve shortcut — RL learns to pick, which is the correct long-term behavior. Existing checkpoints become stale but loadable. Documented as known non-blocking issue (same as 14.1).
</encoding_decision>
